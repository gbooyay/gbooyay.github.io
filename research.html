Lensing of Gravitational Waves and EM Sources

JULY 2024 - PRESENT

As a Bridge Fellow at the University of Texas at Dallas, I research galactic and gravitational physics with Dr. Lindsay King. I'm currently studying gravitational lensing and its effects on both gravitational and electromagnetic signals. With the upcoming launch of LISA (Laser Interferometer Space Antanae), we will be able to observe countless more gravitational wave signal, and the effects of gravitational lensing on those signals are expected to be more common. My current project aims to study the lensed EM and gravitational signals of potential LISA sources, such as compact object binaries and possible host galaxies.

Binary Black Hole Collisions

MAY 2021 - MAY 2024

Working with UT's Center for Gravitational Physics and Dr. Pablo Laguna, I studied binary black hole mergers and their gravitational wave emissions. I used Python to solve for initial parameters of LIGO-observed binary black hole collisions and create models for data visualization. I then used the results to examine gravitational waveforms using the software PyCBC, with the end goal being to expand what information we can learn about binary collisions from their waveform. I‚Äôve also run numerical relativity simulations of binary black hole collision on the TACC supercomputer to help build the second MAYA catalog of waveforms.
